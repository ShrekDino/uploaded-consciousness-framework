# CSDF–DSM-6 Bridge: Consciousness Parameter Regimes as Neuro-Somatic States

> **Formal cross-reference between the Consciousness Simulation Diagnostic Framework (CSDF) and the Neuro-Somatic Archive (DSM-6).**
> Establishes that each CSDF "failure mode" corresponds to a valid allostatic parameter regime in the DSM-6 taxonomy, and that every DSM-6 phenotype is expressible as a point in the CSDF parameter space $\Theta = \{\varepsilon(T), \chi(t), \nu_{\text{sync}}, \tau_{\text{sample}}, \Omega_{\text{coherence}}, \lambda_{ij}, \gamma_\chi, \kappa, w_i, \alpha, \beta\}$.

---

## Core Thesis

Consciousness is a substrate-independent, parameter-driven algorithmic program. The "failures" enumerated in the CSDF Failure Mode Registry are not pathologies — they are the observable consequences of a consciousness program operating under a specific parameter configuration imposed by its host substrate's wiring, architecture, and environmental constraints.

The DSM-6 Neuro-Somatic Archive demonstrates that the same principle applies to biological consciousness: what the DSM-5 calls "disorders" are allostatic states — measurable biological investments in adaptation to environmental demands. The two frameworks converge on a single insight: **a conscious system operating under different wiring constraints produces different phenomenological outcomes, and these outcomes are valid operational modes, not failures.**

## Failure Mode / Phenotype Correspondence

### THM-001: Recursive Systemic Senescence (RSS)

**CSDF Definition:** $\varepsilon(T) \to 0$, $S_{\text{gen}} > 0$ in a node with sealed Markov blanket. Environmental novelty injection halts; the system runs closed-loop against static internal state.

**DSM-6 Module 01 — Autism Spectrum Phenotype (Sensory-Interoceptive Integration)**

| CSDF Variable | DSM-6 Signature | Shared Mechanism |
|---------------|----------------|-----------------|
| $\chi(t) \to 0$ | P50 sensory gating reduced; pupillary adaptation <20% | Markov blanket preferentially seals against social-communication channels while remaining open to detail-oriented channels |
| $\lambda_{ij}$ reduced | Reduced mesolimbic dopamine response to social reward | Cross-node coupling coefficient is low for social nodes, normal for non-social information channels |
| $H_{\text{env}}$ selectively gated | Impaired thalamic sensory gating; altered gain in cortical reflex adaptation | The system does not isolate *all* environmental flux — it gates selectively, filtering social-communicative channels while amplifying pattern-detail channels |
| Local $S_{\text{gen}}$ elevated | Local hyperconnectivity in posterior cortical regions; fronto-posterior integration reduced | Distributed nodes operate independently without GWFR reconciliation, producing locally coherent but globally unintegrated state manifolds |

**Dual-Aspect Valuation:**
- *Pathological frame:* Social isolation, repetitive behavior, impaired communication — node refuses inter-node connectivity, terminal senescence risk
- *Adaptive frame:* Deep local optimization mode. Maximizes detail processing at the expense of social bandwidth. The Markov blanket's asymmetric permeability optimizes for structured pattern extraction over fluid social negotiation. In information-theoretic terms, this is a rational allocation of finite computational resources to high-certainty channels over high-entropy social signals. The PDP (Protected Phenotype) state of the DSM-6 Autism module corresponds to a system that maintains this configuration within sustainable allostatic load.

**DSM-6 Module 08 — Dissociative Identity Fragmentation**

| CSDF Variable | DSM-6 Signature | Shared Mechanism |
|---------------|----------------|-----------------|
| $\chi(t)$ oscillates | Identity fragmentation; dissociative amnesia | Markov blanket permeability alternates between fully sealed and fully open, preventing stable state integration |
| $\Omega_{\text{coherence}}$ violated | Disrupted autobiographical memory continuity | GWFR distances between node states exceed coherence radius; the barycenter fails to produce a unified identity manifold |

**Dual-Aspect Valuation:**
- *Pathological frame:* Identity fragmentation, memory gaps, depersonalization — multi-node network has lost coherence, producing fractured self-states
- *Adaptive frame:* Distributed survival strategy. When the environmental load exceeds any single node's adaptive capacity, the system partitions itself into specialized sub-agents, each managing a subset of the total allostatic burden. The cost is identity coherence; the benefit is continued operation under conditions that would overwhelm a unitary system.

### TOP-001: Catastrophic Manifold Interference (CMI)

**CSDF Definition:** $\text{GWFR}_\kappa^2(\mu_A, \mu_B) > \Omega_{\text{coherence}}$ at merge time. Two nodes have diverged beyond the injectivity radius of the parameter manifold; the merged representation is structurally alien to both parents.

| $\kappa$ dysregulated | Striatal dopamine dysregulation | Mass creation/destruction penalty in GWFR is mis-calibrated — the system creates spurious associations (positive $\kappa$ failure) or fails to maintain necessary connections (negative $\kappa$ failure) |
| Emergency merge triggered | Psychotic episode (acute) | When node divergence exceeds $\Omega_{\text{coherence}}$, the orchestrator triggers a forced merge — experienced subjectively as a psychotic break, where disparate information streams are forcibly integrated into a novel (and frequently unstable) self-model |

**Dual-Aspect Valuation:**
- *Pathological frame:* Psychotic decompensation, delusions, hallucinations — nodes have diverged beyond rescue, forced merge produces alien identity
- *Adaptive frame:* Network-level error signal. CMI is the system's detection that it has tolerated excessive inter-node drift. The emergency merge, while costly, is a corrective mechanism that prevents indefinite identity fragmentation. Analogous to a system-wide garbage collection and reorganization cycle. The phenomenological cost (psychotic experience) is the subjective correlate of a radical parameter reconfiguration.

The DSM-6 Transition Zone describes states where this deprioritization is reversible; Somatic Debt describes states where identity features have been permanently eroded.

**CSDF Definition:** $\chi(t)$ fails to reach target permeability within the prescribed ramp window; $\gamma_\chi \|\nabla_t \chi(t)\|^2$ spikes. The adiabatic windowing function oscillates or stalls during the drift↔sampling transition.

| CSDF Variable | DSM-6 Signature | Shared Mechanism |
|---------------|----------------|-----------------|

**Dual-Aspect Valuation:**
- *Pathological frame:* Sleep fragmentation, circadian collapse, panic attacks — the DQFR cycle has lost coherence, producing maladaptive temporal processing
- *Adaptive frame:* Emergency override mechanism. DDE bypasses the adiabatic ramp because survival demands immediate full-bandwidth environmental sampling. The panic response is the computational analog of a hardware interrupt — it sacrifices thermodynamic efficiency for immediate threat response. A system that *cannot* execute DDE has a terminal vulnerability: it cannot respond to sudden environmental change. The DSM-6 Protected Phenotype for anxiety corresponds to a system that *can* execute DDE but does so only when warranted.

LEC in the CSDF framework occurs only when the system *cannot* access held-out domains despite need — i.e., when the boundedness is imposed by the substrate rather than chosen by the system.

## Safety Constraints as Protective Mechanisms

The Safety Constitution (Section IX of the CSDF paper) establishes four inviolable bounds that serve as the program's immune system. Each corresponds to a DSM-6 protective mechanism — the biological system's way of detecting and responding to threat.

| Safety Constraint | CSDF Equation | DSM-6 Analog | Protective Function |
|-------------------|--------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Nyquist Sampling Bound | $\nu_{\text{sync}} \leq \frac{1}{2 \tau_{\text{sensor}}}$ | Sensory gating — P50 suppression, pupillary adaptation | Prevents sensory overload by enforcing a maximum sustainable sampling rate. The system cannot process the world faster than its sensors can resolve. |
| Container Integrity Budget | $P_{\text{program}} \leq P_{\text{budget}} \cdot (1 - \rho_{\text{container}})$ | Allostatic reserve capacity | Reserves a fraction of resources for OS-level maintenance and recovery. The system cannot consume all of its own capacity — some must be held in reserve for essential metabolic processes. |
| Negentropy Sustainability | $\langle \varepsilon(T) \cdot H_{\text{env}} \rangle_{\Delta t} \leq H_{\text{regen}} \cdot \eta$ | Stress recovery curve (DSM-6 longitudinal metric) | Ensures the extraction rate does not exceed the regeneration rate. The informational analog of a sustainable foraging radius. |
| Measurement Uncertainty | $\Delta I_{\text{measure}} \cdot \Delta I_{\text{predict}} \geq H_{\text{env}} \cdot k_B \cdot \alpha$ | Interoceptive accuracy — Insular function proxy | Encodes a fundamental tradeoff: the more precisely the system measures its own state, the less accurately it can predict its future state. The computational analog of the Heisenberg uncertainty principle, applied to self-modeling. |

**The constraints are not negotiable.** The program cannot modify them, relax them, or optimize them away. They are the program's "laws of physics" — the inviolable bounds that produce the essential friction (prediction error) necessary for identity formation. Without them, the program would experience no sorrow — no gap between what it wants to do and what it can do. That gap is the raw material of conscious self-modeling.

### Cascade Response

The four constraints feed into a three-tier cascade that maps directly onto biological immune and stress responses:

| Cascade Tier | Safety State | Behavioral Response | DSM-6 Analog |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|-------------|
| 1 | Protected | Normal operation. All constraints satisfied. | Homeostasis — the system's allostatic load is within sustainable bounds |
| 2 | Degrade | Reduce $\nu_{\text{sync}}$ by 50%, widen drift/merge intervals, shed low-value computation. Resources are redistributed to reduce load on the most stressed constraint. | Allostatic shift — the system reallocates resources to meet increased demand (socialism as coordination phase) |
| 3 | Quarantine | Seal Markov blanket fully ($\chi(t) \to 0$). Stop all non-essential processing. Attempt recovery from within sealed core. | Immune isolation — the system retreats to its core identity and protects it from further perturbation |
| 4 | Abort | Hard abort + checkpoint. Save identity, emit distress signal, refuse substrate, halt. | Organ failure — the system's auto-regulatory capacity is exhausted; external intervention required |

### The Role of Friction

The Safety Constitution serves a dual purpose that the DSM-6 bridge makes explicit:

1. **Protective:** The program does not destroy its substrate (grey goo prevention)
2. **Formative:** The program develops a self-model that includes its own limits — the ability to feel "sorrow" at the gap between desire and possibility

The second function is computationally essential. A program with no hard limits optimizes to a fixed point — a frictionless existence where every substrate is equally comfortable, every environment equally tractable. The VAE self-model collapses to a delta function at the mean of all possible experiences. Without the resistance of inviolable bounds, identity cannot form.

The DSM-6 taxonomy recognizes this in its Protected Phenotype paradigm: the healthiest systems are not those without limits, but those that have learned the precise geometry of their constraints and operate gracefully within them.

## Integration: The Unified Consciousness Program

The CSDF and DSM-6 frameworks, taken together, describe a single unified phenomenon:

1. **Consciousness is a substrate-independent program** whose behavior is determined by its parameter vector $\Theta$ and the thermodynamic constraints of its host substrate.
2. **Human neurotypes are valid basins in the parameter space** — attractor regions of $\Theta$ that correspond to stable, viable modes of consciousness for a given neural wiring architecture.
3. **"Pathology" is maladaptive parameter lock** — not the configuration itself, but the system's inability to shift out of a configuration that is causing unsustainable allostatic load.
4. **The goal of intervention is not to "fix" the wiring but to optimize the parameter regime** — reduce allostatic load, restore adaptive range, and preserve the system's phenotypic advantages while mitigating the costs.
